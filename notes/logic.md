# logic

**IPA**: /lɒdʒɪk/
**Level**: Level 1

### Collins Definition
1. Logic is a method of reasoning that involves a series of statements, each of which must be true if the statement before it is true.
2. The logic of a conclusion or an argument is its quality of being correct and reasonable.
3. A particular kind of logic is the way of thinking and reasoning about things that is characteristic of a particular type of person or particular field of activity.
4. the branch of philosophy concerned with analysing the patterns of reasoning by which a conclusion is properly drawn from a set of premises, without reference to meaning or context.
5. any particular formal system in which are defined axioms and rules of inference.
6. the system and principles of reasoning used in a specific field of study
7. a particular method of argument or reasoning
8. force or effectiveness in argument or dispute
9. reasoned thought or argument, as distinguished from irrationality
10. the relationship and interdependence of a series of events, facts, etc
11. the principles underlying the units in a computer system that perform arithmetical and logical operations.
12. ( as modifier )
13. the branch of philosophy dealing with correct reasoning, describing relationships among propositions in terms of implication, contradiction, contrariety, conversion, etc.
14. a particular system of such relationships
15. correct reasoning; valid induction or deduction
16. way of reasoning, whether correct or incorrect
17. the system of principles underlying any art or science
18. necessary connection or outcome, as through the working of cause and effect
19. the systematized interconnection of switching functions, circuits, or devices, as in electronic computers
20. the science that investigates the principles governing correct or reliable inference
21. a particular method of reasoning or argumentation
22. the system or principles of reasoning applicable to any branch of knowledge or study
23. reason or sound judgment, as in utterances or actions
24. convincing forcefulness; inexorable truth or persuasiveness
25. a combining form used in the formation of adjectives corresponding to nouns ending in -logy
